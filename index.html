Managed commerce roadmap

How Harito can handle orders

Harito can move from discovery into checkout only after vendor catalogs, payment responsibility, commission rules, delivery ownership, and return handling are agreed. This keeps the business clean before real money or inventory is involved.

01

Vendor catalog

Collect approved SKU data, images, sizes, colours, MRP, selling price, stock, tax, and usage rules.

02

Commercial terms

Agree commission percentage, payout timing, cancellation rules, return deductions, and invoicing format.

03

Harito checkout

Accept buyer payment only for approved vendors, then create an internal order record and vendor order request.

04

Vendor settlement

Pay the vendor their agreed product amount after deducting Harito commission or use a marketplace split flow.

05

Delivery handoff

Either the vendor ships directly or Harito books pickup through a courier partner using vendor pickup details.

06

Returns and reconciliation

Track refunds, RTO, exchanges, courier costs, and final commission before closing the order.

Revenue routes

Terms to negotiate with vendors

To handle payments and delivery directly, Harito needs more than a product list. Each vendor should confirm the commercial model, fulfillment process, return policy, and who is responsible if an order fails.

Commission rate

Define percentage or fixed margin per order, whether GST/taxes are included, and how returns affect commission.

Catalog feed

Request SKU, product title, images, price, stock, size chart, colour, category, HSN/tax, and update frequency.

Payment settlement

Confirm whether Harito pays prepaid, post-delivery, weekly, or after return window closure.

Fulfillment model

Decide vendor direct shipping, Harito courier pickup, packaging ownership, tracking updates, and delivery SLA.

Returns and support

Document who accepts returns, who pays reverse shipping, who handles damaged items, and refund timelines.

Brand usage rules

Confirm logo, product image, discount display, AI recommendation, and ad-copy permissions before publishing.
